What do we offer?

inspire learning

The first five years of life are a window of opportunity when a child’s brain development is at its peak! A child develops physical skills as they learn to crawl, climb, and balance. Dexterity develops when the child handles toys or holds a paintbrush. Language increases as a child plays and interacts with others. Learning to cooperate, negotiate, take turns, and play by the rules are all important skills that a child will practice and learn during a visit to the Museum.

enhance education

Young children are driven to explore and by 6 months they are using all of their senses to learn about their world. Just like our exhibits, our education programs engage children from infancy through elementary school with age-appropriate experiences in math, science, relationship building, empathy and compassion, art and creativity. We take our classes directly to schools and libraries and deliver them onsite to field trip groups.

connect community

We are here to meet the needs of children and families in the Boulder County community. We seek community input and participation in our exhibit and program development; and strive to make the Museum as accessible as possible. Our Play for All Initiative provides support to over 6,000 visitors every year.
